DAE First Year Mathematics Syllabus

Total Contact Hours

Theory: 64 | Practical: 32

Learning Outcomes

  • Develop a strong foundation in basic mathematical concepts and techniques.
  • Apply mathematical principles to solve problems in engineering and technical fields.
  • Enhance analytical and problem-solving skills through algebraic and geometric methods.
  • Prepare for advanced mathematical applications in subsequent years of the DAE program.

Course Outline

1. Algebra 15 Hrs

1.1 Introduction to basic algebraic expressions and operations.

1.2 Simplification of algebraic fractions and factorization.

1.3 Linear equations and their solutions.

1.4 Quadratic equations: Methods of solving (factoring, completing the square, quadratic formula).

1.5 Systems of linear equations with two variables (substitution and elimination methods).

1.6 Inequalities and their graphical representation.

1.7 Sequences and series (arithmetic and geometric progressions).

1.8 Binomial theorem and its applications.

2. Trigonometry 12 Hrs

2.1 Measurement of angles (degrees and radians).

2.2 Trigonometric ratios and their values for standard angles.

2.3 Trigonometric identities and proofs.

2.4 Solution of right-angled triangles.

2.5 Law of Sines and Law of Cosines.

2.6 Heights and distances (practical applications).

2.7 Graphs of trigonometric functions (sine, cosine, tangent).

3. Geometry 10 Hrs

3.1 Basic geometric shapes and properties (lines, angles, triangles, quadrilaterals).

3.2 Theorems of congruence and similarity of triangles.

3.3 Circle geometry: Chords, tangents, and secants.

3.4 Coordinate geometry: Distance and midpoint formulas.

3.5 Equation of a straight line and its slope.

3.6 Introduction to conic sections (circle, ellipse, parabola).

4. Mensuration 08 Hrs

4.1 Area and perimeter of plane figures (triangle, rectangle, circle).

4.2 Surface area and volume of solids (cube, cuboid, cylinder, sphere).

4.3 Practical problems involving mensuration in engineering designs.

4.4 Conversion of units and scaling factors.

5. Calculus (Introduction) 10 Hrs

5.1 Limits and continuity (basic concepts).

5.2 Differentiation: Rules and applications (product, quotient, chain rule).

5.3 Applications of derivatives (rate of change, maxima, and minima).

5.4 Introduction to integration (basic integrals and their applications).

5.5 Area under curves (simple cases).

6. Matrices and Determinants 06 Hrs

6.1 Definition and types of matrices.

6.2 Operations on matrices (addition, subtraction, multiplication).

6.3 Determinants and their properties.

6.4 Solution of linear equations using matrices.

7. Statistics (Basic) 05 Hrs

7.1 Collection and presentation of data (tables, graphs).

7.2 Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode).

7.3 Measures of dispersion (range, variance, standard deviation).

8. Practical Applications 08 Hrs

8.1 Solving real-world engineering problems using algebra.

8.2 Use of trigonometric tables and calculators.

8.3 Geometric constructions with tools.

8.4 Numerical problems on mensuration and calculus.

8.5 Group projects on statistical data analysis.

Recommended Books

  • 1. Higher Engineering Mathematics by B.S. Grewal.
  • 2. Elementary Mathematics for Engineers by H.K. Dass.
  • 3. Calculus and Analytic Geometry by Thomas and Finney.
  • 4. A Textbook of Mathematics for Polytechnics by R.D. Sharma.
  • 5. Practical Mathematics for Technical Students by George S. Best.
