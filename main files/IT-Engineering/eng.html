DAE First Year English Syllabus

Total Contact Hours

Theory: 48 | Practical: 32

Learning Outcomes

  • Develop proficiency in reading, writing, listening, and speaking skills.
  • Understand and apply basic grammar rules in communication.
  • Enhance vocabulary and comprehension for technical and general contexts.
  • Prepare for effective written and oral communication in professional settings.

Course Outline

1. Introduction to English Language 03 Hrs

1.1 Importance of English in technical education.

1.2 Basic language skills: Listening, Speaking, Reading, Writing.

1.3 Overview of the course structure.

2. Grammar Fundamentals 10 Hrs

2.1 Parts of Speech (Nouns, Pronouns, Verbs, etc.).

2.2 Sentence Structure and Types.

2.3 Tenses (Present, Past, Future).

2.4 Articles and Prepositions.

2.5 Active and Passive Voice.

3. Vocabulary Building 06 Hrs

3.1 Common words and phrases in technical contexts.

3.2 Synonyms, Antonyms, and Homonyms.

3.3 Use of dictionaries and thesauri.

4. Reading Comprehension 08 Hrs

4.1 Understanding short passages and technical texts.

4.2 Identifying main ideas and supporting details.

4.3 Answering comprehension questions.

5. Writing Skills 10 Hrs

5.1 Paragraph writing (Descriptive, Narrative).

5.2 Letter writing (Formal and Informal).

5.3 Report writing basics.

5.4 Punctuation and capitalization rules.

6. Oral Communication 06 Hrs

6.1 Basic conversation skills.

6.2 Pronunciation and intonation practice.

6.3 Group discussions and presentations.

7. Practical Applications 05 Hrs

7.1 Role-playing technical scenarios.

7.2 Listening to audio materials and note-taking.

7.3 Peer review of written assignments.

Recommended Books

  • 1. English Grammar in Use by Raymond Murphy.
  • 2. Practical English Usage by Michael Swan.
  • 3. Technical Communication by John M. Lannon.
  • 4. Oxford English for Technical Purposes.
