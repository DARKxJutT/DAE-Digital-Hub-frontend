DAE First Year Electronics Syllabus

Total Contact Hours

Theory: 48 | Practical: 48

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the fundamental principles of electronics and their practical applications.
  • Develop skills in assembling, testing, and troubleshooting electronic circuits.
  • Apply theoretical concepts to real-world electronics projects and designs.
  • Prepare for advanced studies in electronics and related engineering fields.

Course Outline

1. Introduction to Electronics 06 Hrs

1.1 Definition and historical development of electronics.

1.2 Overview of electronic components (resistors, capacitors, inductors, diodes).

1.3 Basic electrical concepts (voltage, current, resistance, power).

1.4 Safety protocols and equipment handling in electronics labs.

2. Semiconductor Fundamentals 10 Hrs

2.1 Introduction to semiconductors and their properties.

2.2 P-type and N-type materials: Doping process.

2.3 Diode operation: PN junction, forward and reverse bias.

2.4 Bipolar Junction Transistors (BJT): NPN and PNP configurations.

2.5 Field Effect Transistors (FET): JFET and MOSFET basics.

2.6 Practical testing and identification of semiconductor devices.

3. Basic Electronic Circuits 12 Hrs

3.1 Ohm’s Law and its applications in circuit analysis.

3.2 Kirchhoff’s Voltage and Current Laws (KVL, KCL).

3.3 Series and parallel circuit configurations.

3.4 RC circuits: Time constants and transient response.

3.5 RL circuits: Energy storage and decay.

3.6 Introduction to amplifiers (common emitter, common base).

3.7 Basic oscillator circuits and their principles.

3.8 Troubleshooting techniques for circuit faults.

4. Digital Electronics 08 Hrs

4.1 Binary, decimal, and hexadecimal number systems.

4.2 Logic gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR operations.

4.3 Boolean algebra: Laws and simplification methods.

4.4 Combinational logic circuits (adders, subtractors).

4.5 Sequential logic: Flip-flops (SR, JK, D, T).

4.6 Introduction to counters and shift registers.

5. Electronic Measurement and Instruments 06 Hrs

5.1 Use of multimeters for voltage, current, and resistance measurement.

5.2 Operation of oscilloscopes: Waveform analysis.

5.3 Introduction to function generators and signal analysis.

5.4 Calibration and maintenance of electronic instruments.

6. Practical Electronics and Projects 06 Hrs

6.1 Assembly of a simple power supply circuit.

6.2 Soldering techniques and printed circuit board (PCB) design.

6.3 Building and testing a basic amplifier circuit.

6.4 Group project: Design a simple electronic timer (due by 02:10 PM PKT, Wednesday, June 04, 2025).

Recommended Books

  • 1. "Electronic Principles" by Albert Malvino and David Bates.
  • 2. "Fundamentals of Electric Circuits" by Charles K. Alexander and Matthew Sadiku.
  • 3. "Digital Electronics: Principles and Applications" by Roger L. Tokheim.
  • 4. "Basic Electronics for Scientists and Engineers" by Dennis L. Eggleston.
