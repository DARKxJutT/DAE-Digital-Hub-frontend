DAE First Year Electronics Syllabus

Total Contact Hours

Theory: 48 | Practical: 48

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the basic principles of electronics and their applications.
  • Develop skills in handling electronic components and circuits.

    • 3. Apply theoretical knowledge to practical electronics projects.

  • Prepare for advanced electronics studies in subsequent years.

Course Outline

1. Introduction to Electronics 06 Hrs

1.1 Definition and scope of electronics.

1.2 Overview of electronic components (resistors, capacitors, diodes).

1.3 Basic electrical quantities (voltage, current, resistance).

1.4 Safety practices in electronics labs.

2. Semiconductor Devices 10 Hrs

2.1 Introduction to semiconductors (P-type, N-type).

2.2 Diode characteristics and applications.

2.3 Bipolar Junction Transistors (BJT): Operation and configurations.

2.4 Field Effect Transistors (FET): Basics and uses.

2.5 Practical testing of semiconductor devices.

3. Basic Electronic Circuits 10 Hrs

3.1 Ohm’s Law and Kirchhoff’s Laws.

3.2 Series and parallel circuits.

3.3 RC and RL circuits (time constants).

3.4 Introduction to amplifiers and oscillators.

3.5 Troubleshooting basic circuits.

4. Digital Electronics Basics 08 Hrs

4.1 Binary number system and conversions.

4.2 Logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR).

4.3 Boolean algebra and simplification techniques.

4.4 Introduction to flip-flops and counters.

5. Electronic Instrumentation 06 Hrs

5.1 Use of multimeters and oscilloscopes.

5.2 Measurement of voltage, current, and resistance.

5.3 Introduction to signal generators.

6. Practical Electronics 08 Hrs

6.1 Assembly of simple electronic circuits.

6.2 Soldering techniques and PCB design basics.

6.3 Testing and debugging of circuits.

6.4 Group project on a basic electronic device.

Recommended Books

  • 1. "Electronic Principles" by Albert Malvino and David Bates.
  • 2. "Fundamentals of Electric Circuits" by Charles K. Alexander and Matthew Sadiku.
  • 3. "Digital Fundamentals" by Thomas L. Floyd.
  • 4. "Basic Electronics for Engineers" by Dennis Eggleston.
