General Questions

1. What is the DAE Digital Library?
The DAE Digital Library is an online platform providing access to digital resources including e-books, journals, research papers, and multimedia content.
Who can access the DAE Digital Library?
The library is available to all currently enrolled DAE students, faculty members, and staff with valid institutional credentials.
Is there a mobile app for the library?
Yes, But in its development Phase.

How do I contact library support?

Are there any tutorials for using the library?
Yes, we offer video tutorials, interactive guides, and weekly webinars. Visit our "Help & Tutorials" section to access these resources.
Can I access library resources after graduation?
Use your institutional username and password (the same credentials you use for the student portal or campus WiFi).

Yes, you can access all resources remotely by logging in with your institutional credentials. Some resources may require VPN for full access.
Why am I getting "Access Denied"?
This typically means your account is not active or you're not properly authenticated. Contact the IT helpdesk if the issue persists after trying to log out and back in.
Does the library offer research assistance?
Yes, our librarians provide research consultations, citation help, and database search strategies. Book an appointment through your account dashboard.
Are there any late fees for digital resources?
No late fees, but some materials may become automatically unavailable after the loan period expires. Some items may have limited simultaneous users.

4. Why does my session keep timing out?
For security, sessions expire after 60 minutes of inactivity. You can enable "Keep me logged in" for longer sessions on personal devices.

Yes, you can be logged in on up to 5 devices simultaneously. Some high-demand resources may limit concurrent access.
6. What if I'm having trouble with two-factor authentication?
Contact the IT helpdesk for assistance with 2FA issues. Have your student ID number ready when you call.
7. Do I need VPN to access all resources?
Most resources don't require VPN, but some publisher content may need it. The system will prompt you if VPN is required.
8. How do I access restricted databases?
Restricted databases require special permissions. Faculty can request access; students need faculty sponsorship for these resources.
9. Can visiting scholars get library access?
Yes, visiting scholars can obtain temporary credentials through their host department. Allow 3-5 business days for setup.
10. Why can't I access some resources from my country?
Some publishers impose geographic restrictions. Try using our VPN service or contact us for alternative access options.

We support Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, and Opera in their most recent versions.

9. How do I configure my ebook reader?
Visit our "Ebook Help" section for device-specific setup guides. Most readers work with Adobe Digital Editions.
10. What download formats are available?
Most articles offer PDF, while ebooks may provide EPUB or PDF. Some videos allow MP4 download for offline viewing.

Copyright & Usage

Can I share resources with classmates?
You may share permalinks to resources, but distributing downloaded copies may violate copyright and license agreements. Encourage others to access through the library.
10. How do I get permission to reuse content?
Use our Copyright Clearance Service or contact the publisher directly for reuse permissions beyond fair use.
What are the copyright restrictions?
Resources are for personal academic use only. Commercial use, redistribution, or systematic downloading is prohibited. Always check the terms for each resource.
Can I print chapters from ebooks?
Most ebooks allow limited printing (usually 10-20% of content) for personal study purposes. The platform will warn you if you exceed allowed limits.
